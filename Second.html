Cultivating Lion's Mane Mushrooms

Inoculation Onto Agar

H. erinaceus mycelia growth on nutrified agar media via tissue culture.

Liquid Culture:

The agar culture is expanded into a nutrified liquid medium. Expanding the growth exponentially.

Liquid Culture includes water, honey, nutritional yeast, soy peptone, and whole oats. After 15 days the liquid culture is ready to inoculate sterilized grain spawn.

Grain Inoculation

Sterilized whole oats are inoculated with 30ml of liquid culture. The grains will then be colonized by the mushroom mycelium, 20 days after innoculation.

Once colonized, grain must be expanded into more grain, or transfered to fruiting substrate.

Fruiting Blocks

The final step of the process is to inoculate a blend of hardwood and soybean hulls, with the colonized grain spawn.

After 25 days, the colonized fruiting blocks are forming primordia, and are ready to be introduced to fruiting conditions. Mushrooms will take 2-3 weeks to fully form. And will continue to produce mushrooms until the nutrients in the fruiting substrate are consumed.
