Cultivating Lion's Mane Mushrooms

Innoculation Onto Agar

H. erinaceus mycelia growth on nutrified agar media via tissue culture.

Common names:

  • Lion's Mane
  • Monkey's Head
  • Sheep's Head
  • Bear's Head
  • Old Man's Beard
  • Satyr's Beard
  • Pom Pom
  • Houtou
  • Yamabushi-take (Japanese for "Mountain-Priest Mushroom"

Liquid Culture:

The agar culture is expanded into a nutrified liquid medium. Expanding the growth exponentially.

Liquid Culture includes water, honey, nutritional yeast, soy peptone, and whole oats.

After 15 days the liquid culture is ready to innoculate sterilized grain spawn.

Grain Innoculation

Sterilized whole oats are innoculated with 30ml of liquid culture. The grains will then be colonized by the mushroom mycelium in 20 days after innoculation.

Once colonized grain must be expanded into more grain, or transfered to fruiting substrate.

Fruiting Blocks

The final step to the process is to innoculate a blend of hardwood and soybean hulls, with the colonized grain spawn.

After 25 days, the colonized fruiting blocks are forming primordia, and are ready to be introduced to fruiting conditions.

Mushrooms will take 2-3 weeks to fully form. And will continue to produce mushrooms until the nutrients in the fruiting substrate are consumed.
